# Concept Taxonomy

## Categories

### 1. Foundation Concepts (FOUND)
Core mathematical building blocks: relations, functions, domain, range, variables, coordinate plane, number systems, and basic notation.

### 2. Linear Functions (LIN)
Straight lines, slope/gradient, forms of linear equations, parallel and perpendicular lines, simultaneous equations, and linear models.

### 3. Graphing and Visualization (GRAPH)
Technology use, sketching vs drawing, key graph features, window settings, intercepts, turning points, and graphical methods.

### 4. Quadratic Functions (QUAD)
Parabolas, quadratic forms (standard, factored, vertex), discriminant, nature of roots, solving techniques, and quadratic applications.

### 5. Inverse and Composite (INVC)
Inverse functions, one-to-one functions, horizontal line test, composite functions, composition rules, and decomposing functions.

### 6. Rational Functions (RAT)
Reciprocal function, hyperbola, asymptotes (vertical, horizontal, oblique), rational forms, domain restrictions, and holes.

### 7. Exponential Functions (EXP)
Exponential growth and decay, bases, Euler's number, natural exponential, exponential equations, and real-world exponential models.

### 8. Logarithmic Functions (LOG)
Logarithms (common, natural), logarithm laws, change of base, solving log equations, log-exponential inverse relationship, and logarithmic scale.

### 9. Polynomial Functions (POLY)
Higher-degree polynomials, factor and remainder theorems, polynomial division, root properties, sum/product of roots, and end behavior.

### 10. Function Classifications (CLASS)
Even/odd functions, symmetry testing, periodic functions, modulus function, absolute value, piecewise functions, and function behavior analysis.

### 11. Transformations (TRANS)
Translations, reflections, stretches, scale factors, composite transformations, transformation notation, and effect on coordinates.

### 12. Algebra Foundations (ALG)
Expressions, equations, inequalities, algebraic manipulation, factoring techniques, expanding, and collecting like terms.

### 13. Modeling and Applications (MODEL)
Function modeling, regression, curve fitting, interpolation, extrapolation, mathematical modeling cycle, units, and significant figures.
